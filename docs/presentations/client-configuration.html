Product update · Ecometry

MSRP grew up.
Meet Client Configuration.

A single price bolted to a SKU couldn’t capture how clients really price across channels. So we turned it into a flexible Client Configuration — set across four distribution levels, carrying far more than a price.

Ecometry → Product → Client SKUs → Client Configuration · Press Autoplay to watch.
01 Before

One SKU, one price.

MSRP lived directly on the client SKU — a single price + currency per product. Simple… until real-world pricing showed up.

  • Just one MSRP per SKU — no way to vary by channel.
  • The price also lived on Client SKU Regions — two homes, drifting apart.

02 The problem

The same SKU, priced four different ways.

Clients across business units (FSA · CMI · DSM) price by distribution channel: a global default, a per-region price, a per-store price — or a price for one store within a region. One field can’t hold four truths.

Global

One value for the whole country / all channels.

MX$ 32.00
Region

A value for one region of a region-system.

MX$ 31.00
Store

A value for a specific store / banner.

MX$ 30.00
Region & Store

A store within a region — the most specific.

MX$ 29.00
03 The idea

A new entity, not a new column.

Instead of bolting three more prices onto the SKU, we attached an independent Client Configuration that varies across four distribution levels — each an overlay that can override the ones above it.

  • Client SKU → many Client Configuration rows (one per level).
  • Four levels: Global · Region · Store · Region & Store.

05 The rule that keeps it safe

The rightmost defined value always wins.

Level 1Global
Level 2Region
Level 3Store
Level 4Region & Store
  • Region overwrites Global · Store overwrites Region · Region & Store overwrites Store.
  • Levels inherit, they’re not exclusive — Global is the floor, Region & Store is the final word.
06 Why the rename

It stopped being a price. So it stopped being called MSRP.

Business unit, client category, hero and validity dates all vary by the same four levels — so they joined MSRP on the entity. A “price” became a configuration.

08 Under the hood

Renamed tables, repointed pipelines.

The four tables follow the name, and the data platform follows the tables.

  • Repoint Airbyte sources & the dbt/ETL pipeline to the new tables.

09 In short

One price, two homes → one home, four contexts.

Flexible by dimension

Price a SKU globally, by region, by store, or by store-within-a-region.

Safe precedence

Global → Region → Store → Region & Store — the rightmost value wins.

More than a price

Business unit, client category, hero & validity live at every level.

One source of truth

No more duplicated regional prices drifting apart.
