Product update · Ecometry

Pricing & merchandising,
now down to the store.

The commercial settings on a product — MSRP, hero, business unit and client category — used to be set once, for the whole country. Now they can be configured by region, by store, and by store within a region. Global stays exactly as it was — store is the big new lever.

Ecometry → Product → Client SKUs → Client Configuration · Press Autoplay to watch.
01 Until now

One setting for the whole country.

A product’s price, hero status, business unit and client category were set a single time — and applied everywhere. Every region, every store, the same values.

  • No way to price a product differently in one region or one store.
  • Real distribution deals didn’t fit — overrides were manual and messy.

05 More than a price

It’s the whole commercial setup — per level.

Every level carries more than the price. A product can be a hero in one store and not another, sit in a different client category by region, or map to a different business unit — each with its own validity dates.

Set at every level

Price is now optional — a level can carry just the hero flag, category or business unit, with no price at all.
06 Why it matters

Reflect how each client actually sells.

Price by store

Match the exact price a client sells at — store by store, not just a country default.

Hero where it counts

Flag a product as hero only in the stores or regions where it really is one.

Fits real deals

Regional and store agreements finally map one-to-one onto the platform.

Less manual work

Set it once at the right level; the closest setting applies automatically — no juggling overrides.
